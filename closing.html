Closing Thoughts

Final reflections and takeaways

My Research Topic: Aerospace Engineering in Modern Warfare

Why this topic resonates with my engineering mindset

01

Connecting Geopolitics and Technology

The motivation behind my study on the evolution of aerospace engineering directed at supporting modern warfare was directly related to my interest in systems and the accompanying complex interdependencies viewed as systems. Just as I analyze the performance of mechanical systems under stress, my interest also peaked at examining how the engine behind global geopolitical tensions can act as a significant catalyst to shift an entire field of study and space of work. The article I examined provided a compelling case study of how innovation is impacted by conflict, especially in the field of UAVs and AI-based defense systems. This is similar to how I consistently experience structured thinking under tension, when external urgency requires me to think on the fly and make decisions under pressure.

02

Beyond the Battlefield: Civilian Applications

What struck me with this research was the understanding that the military drive to produce technology can create awesome civilian applications. Autonomous vehicles, AI-based navigation, and high performance materials are not limited to the war theatre; they have incredible potential uses in civilian logistics, farming and emergency services. This duality strengthens my belief that engineering can be a process of creation that can benefit society in many different ways, possibly with no initial intention at all. Building resilient systems to serve multiple purposes, similar to the things I try to produce in my own problem solving.

03

Why Indigenous Innovation Matters

The article's emphasis on indigenous innovation particularly resonated with my systems-thinking approach. Relying on external technologies creates vulnerabilities within a system, akin to a single point of failure. The drive to develop local capabilities, as highlighted in the research, is a strategic imperative that ensures resilience and self-sufficiency. This concept extends beyond national security to any complex system, whether it's a drone or a personal problem-solving framework: reducing external dependencies strengthens the overall system. This research reinforced my conviction that true engineering mastery involves not just understanding existing solutions, but also the capacity to create novel, self-sustaining ones.
