Reflection on Personal Narrative 1

After examining my first draft, I quickly identified two areas which needed more emphasis; rhetorical elements, and personal reflection. Although the narrative illustrated key events, it didnâ€™t quite convey what I had learned or how I was processing the moment. At times it also felt vague, lacking the sensory detail that allows a reader to connect with the story.

To create a more compelling narrative, I had to add more detailed reflections and rhetorical devices. I wanted the reader to experience the moment with me, not just know what occurred. For example, when recording my attempts to enhance a drone, I included everything in my mind including, the irritation that I was feeling during, and after, the process, as well as what I was doing to make sense of the outcomes. I believe these choices began to add emotional weight to the narrative.

I also transformed the narrative into a more descriptive and specific account, hoping to engage the reader more to live the experience alongside me, rather than just read about it. By presenting my deliberations and responses, I could make the narrative more reflective and honest.

The final draft feels far more engaging. The focus is no longer just a recount of the series of events, but a snapshot of my evolution, a product of the way I was thinking, and the lessons I took from those moments.
