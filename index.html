About Me

The Engineer's Mindset

My approach to problem-solving was made clear during a moment of crisis. When my brother went missing one evening, I didn't panic. Instead, I built a mental control system: scanning for signals, eliminating variables, tracking data points. Like a ground crew running diagnostics.

Systems Under Pressure

That night taught me that engineering isn't just about putting metals together. It's about structure, analysis, and troubleshooting under duress. It's about building frameworks that function when everything else fails.

Aerospace Passion

Why aerospace? Because it represents the ultimate convergence of function, motion, logic, and timing. Every system must work flawlessly, every component must serve its purpose, and every decision must be calculated and precise.
