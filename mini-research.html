Mini Research

Exploring the evolution of aerospace engineering in modern warfare

Research Citation

Thakur, Dr. Amit Kumar. "Rising Above: How Modern Warfare Is Redefining Aerospace Engineering Careers." LPU Blog, Lovely Professional University, 7 July 2025, www.lpu.in/blog/rising-above-how-modern-warfare-is-redefining-aerospace-engineering-careers/

Article Summary

This article discusses the ways in which aerospace engineering jobs have evolved since India and Pakistan's previous war. According to the author, Dr. Amit Kumar Thakur, the war has pushed the need to advance space technology more strongly, particularly in UAVs (Unmanned Aerial Vehicles) and defense systems based on Artificial Intelligence to counter enemy movements.

We can see from the article how classical aerospace engineering has evolved from simply designing aircraft to being a vital component of national defense policies. The emphasis, according to the article, is on employing AI-based drones for spying, inspection, and aerial assaults, with the aim of making these technologies locally so that we are not dependent on foreigners.

The UAVs are described as tools transformed from auxiliary tools to core combat tools, ranging from combat drones and battlefield surveillance systems to reconnaissance nano-drones. The education path starts with a B. Tech in aerospace engineering, followed by specialized master's degrees in areas like AI, UAVs, and autonomous defense systems.

Universities like Lovely Professional University (LPU) in Punjab are leading the wave of new aerospace technologies. Students learn key technical skills such as simulation software, programming languages, and drone control systems. Career opportunities pass across government agencies like DRDO and HAL, companies like IdeaForge and Garuda Aerospace, and emerging deep tech startups.

The article predicts that by 2030, India will create over 100,000 space related jobs and become a global leader in UAV technology.

Personal Assessment and Analysis

Geopolitical Impact on Engineering

This article demonstrates how geopolitical tensions reshape entire fields of scholarship and work. The career shift of the aerospace engineer outlined here is reflective of larger trends for how modern war drives technological innovation and the creation of new job categories.

UAV Technology Evolution

This focus on the UAV technology is a shift in military planning and technical priorities. Pakistan has long taken pride in having the largest stock of unmanned air vehicles (UAVs) in South Asia, owning UAVs largely from Turkey and China, as the only country in this region to deploy drones as a weapon of war.

Civilian Applications

These applications do not only have military applications. Aerospace engineers working on autonomous vehicles, AI-driven navigation, and high-performance materials will have applications across the civilian logistics, agricultural, and emergency service sectors.

Indigenous Innovation

The paper's focus on indigenous innovation addresses a strategic vulnerability, and the countries reliant on purchased technologies, are all vulnerable to disruptions in supply due to war. It gives opportunities to the engineer who is proficient in creating home grown alternatives.

Employment Growth Projections

While the employment growth numbers are somewhat ambitious, they are in line with growth worldwide. Aero India, presumably 2025 was held in Bengaluru, February 10th to 14th and has now locked in India's move to redefine modern warfare.

Skills Framework Evolution

The Skills Framework, which combines classical engineering with AI, robots, and cyber security portrays the integrated character of aerospace activity. This demonstrates the deeper challenges for "traditional education" of engineers.
